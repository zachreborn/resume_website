.Technology Philosophy

Simple

Technology should make our lives simpler, not harder. People follow the path of least resistance, and that means complexity or difficulty leads to ineffective technology. Don't settle for technology that solves one problem but creates another. Utilize the right technology to create efficiency and simplicity.

Secure

Security must be so interwoven into technology that it is indiscernible from the technology itself. It should be a cornerstone for all design and architecture decisions. Security is not a feature that can be added later; it must be built in from the start.

Transparent

Technology should be well-documented and communicated to describe how it works. The most utilized technologies are those which are plainly written, diagrammed, and described. Doing so allows others to use the technology as a building block.

Automated

If it isn't automated, it isn't true. We're all human, and any mundane process is prone to error. Automation of the mundane and the boring solves problems and common mistakes such as typos or entering data into the wrong field. It allows people to focus on higher-level projects, innovation, and growth.

Redundant and Resilient

Technology should be scalable and built for failure. Scalability considered both expansion and contraction. Failure can exist at any layer and when the design of technology accounts for failure that allows for continuity. It is the difference between stressful firefighting and confidence in knowing the technology will work when needed.
